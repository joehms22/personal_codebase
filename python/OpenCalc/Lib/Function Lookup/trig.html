Overview
Trigonometry is a branch of mathematics that studies triangles, particularly right triangles. Trigonometry deals with relationships between the sides and the angles of triangles and with the trigonometric functions, which describe those relationships, as well as describing angles in general and the motion of waves such as sound and light waves. Source: http://www.wikipedia.org

Where is Pi?
Rather than entering 3.14159... OpenCalc provides a variable, PI, to use in your functions in place of π. 
Example:
==sin(PI)
0.0
(If you are in radians)

Sin/Cos/Tan
To get the value of a sine cosine or tangent use:
  • sin(number)
  • cos(number)
  • tan(number)

Inverse/Arc Functions
To get the value of a arcsine cosine or tangent use:
  • asin(number)
  • acos(number)
  • atan(number)

Conversion Functions
OpenCalc provides conversion functions for when you need to convert radians to degrees, or vice versa. 
Radians to degrees:
todeg(number in radians)

Degrees to radians:
torad(number in degrees)

Change to radians or degrees
You may need to change the caulculator from using degrees (default) to radians in some of your endevaours. There is an easy way to do this. 
To change from degrees to radians type:

==useradians()
Now using Radians

To change from radians to degrees type:

==usedegrees()
Now using Degrees
